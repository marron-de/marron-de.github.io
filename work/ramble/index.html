        • Flip the switch back from B to A. Remember to note your new combination.

        if you need to change the combination again, repeat these steps.

    • Q.

      Dual Monitor set up for Apple Products

        To use your Display Link adapter and extend your Mac’s display across multiple screens, follow these steps:

        Step 1: Download the DisplayLink Driver
        1. Visit https://www.synaptics.com/products/displaylink-graphics/downloads/macos
        2. Under the macOS section, select the latest DisplayLink driver compatible with your macOS version.
        3. Click Download and save the file to your Mac.

        Step 2: Install the DisplayLink Driver
        1. 1. Open the downloaded .dmg file.
        2. Double-click the DisplayLink Installer and follow the on-screen instructions.
        3. When prompted, grant permissions to install third-party software by entering your Mac’s administrator password.

        Step 3: Grant Screen Recording Permissions
        macOS requires additional permissions for DisplayLink to function properly:
        1. After installation, go to System Settings (macOS Ventura and later) or System Preferences (macOS Monterey and earlier).
        2. Navigate to Privacy & Security > Screen Recording.
        3. Find DisplayLink Manager in the list and enable the toggle switch.
        4. You may need to restart your Mac for the changes to take effect.

        Step 4: Connect Your DisplayLink Adapter and Monitors
        1. Plug the DisplayLink adapter into your Mac’s USB-C or USB-A port.
        2. Connect your external monitors to the adapter via HDMI, DisplayPort, or VGA, depending on your adapter model.
        3. Your Mac should automatically detect the additional displays. If not, go to System Settings > Displays and arrange your screens as needed.

        Step 5: Enable DisplayLink Manager (macOS Ventura & Later)
        1. Open DisplayLink Manager from the Applications folder or the menu bar.
        2. Ensure that "Launch Automatically After Login" is enabled for seamless operation.

        Troubleshooting Tips:
        • If your external displays are not detected, try unplugging and reconnecting the adapter.
        • Make sure your macOS version is supported by the installed DisplayLink driver.
        • Restart your Mac after installation if displays are not extending properly.
        • Ensure that Screen Recording permissions are enabled for DisplayLink Manager.

        After completing these steps, you’ll be able to extend your Mac’s display across multiple screens using DisplayLink technology.
